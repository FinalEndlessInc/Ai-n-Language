Difficulties in language learning

Learning a new language can be difficult. Especially when traditional methods of teaching have limitations that make it harder for students to succeed. Textbooks, videos, and audio recordings include useful content but lack personalization. Occasionally, this may result in students progressing to the next level before they thoroughly understand the material.



Millions of people worldwide strive to learn a new language, but very few of them end up speaking it fluently. The reasons that people give up learning languages mid-way are several, but here are the most common ones:


  • Lack of motivation:After a couple of lessons, many people feel like they've bitten off more than they can chew, and it's not as much fun as they imagined it to be. As the topics and exercises increase in difficulty, people struggle to keep up and eventually drop out of the program.

  • Language barrier: Most students are afraid of making mistakes while speaking, while the best way to improve is by learning from them. The inability to communicate with natives of a particular language makes it difficult to practice what they've learned.

  • Not focusing on grammar: While it's true that knowing basic tenses can help you converse more or less easily, you can face challenges in some situations when you don't have the necessary grammar knowledge.



How can AI help?

Just as the rise of online learning revolutionized the language learning process, developments in AI technology promise a new turning point. Language learning with AI is becoming increasingly common, as AI enables various useful features in apps and programs.



Language bots

Many believe that to fully understand the peculiarities of a language, you must engage with native speakers. However, AI models replicate real speech and conversations with rapidly increasing accuracy. A language learning chatbot can respond to messages with personalized, relevant information. Language learners can use a chatbot to practice conversations without the anxiety they may feel when talking to a person.


Machine translation

AI technologies such as neural machine translation improve the quality of translations and help with learning foreign languages. One method known as machine translation as a bad model helps students find and fix mistakes in the machine-translated text. Ultimately, this allows students to learn more about the language and improve comprehension, writing, and vocabulary.


Personalized textbooks

Personalized textbooks adapt to learners' needs based on individual progress. Textbook customization is of great value to teachers. Teachers can upload content into an AI system, which can then generate textbooks tailored to a specific school, course, or group of students.



Application of AI in language learning apps

Duolingo


Duolingo is the most popular online language learning app in the world and has more than 300 million active users. It offers more than 100 courses in 38 languages and heavily relies on AI to deliver its personalized courses.

Duolingo has AI chatbots that you can text with to improve your skills. The algorithm is trained to understand the context and provide learners with real-time answers. Their latest integration also involves speech recognition in language learning that teaches the correct pronunciation of words. The learner uses the microphone to record their speech and sends it to the chatbot. The algorithm uses benchmarks like references from native speakers' voices and points out the mistakes in pronunciation.

Duolingo's site: https://en.duolingo.com/

Babbel


Babbel is a German language learning app and an e-learning platform that offers courses in 14 languages. The company has more than 20 million current active users and focuses on using real-life situations and review sessions to foster the language learning process.

Similar to Duolingo, Babbel also uses speech recognition technology to facilitate correct pronunciation and integrates personalized learning activities to engage users. Additionally, the app offers an online test for users to assess their listening and reading skills, and smart AI algorithms grade these tests.

Babbel's site: https://www.babbel.com/?locale=en

Memrise


Its uniqueness lies in its real-time object recognition system. This tool allows users to take pictures of different objects and discover what they're called in a particular language on the app. The users simply need to click on “Explore” mode and point their camera towards an object. Memrise's AI algorithm will recognize it and identify the object's name on the screen.

Memrise also pushes a personalized learning approach to the front with its chatbots. These bots are programmed to carry on real-life conversations with users who want to practice their skills. They can correct your grammar mistakes during conversations and offer new vocabulary that can be used within the scope of particular topics.

Memrise's site: https://www.memrise.com/

Busuu


Busuu is a language learning app developed in Spain that offers courses in 12 different languages to over 100 million users worldwide. Busuu combines AI-powered language learning with one-to-one live tutoring to deliver a full-scale language learning experience to its users.

Recently, Busuu also launched a VR app for learning Spanish on Oculus Gear and Oculus Go, as well as a comprehension test for Amazon's Alexa platform. All you have to do is say, “Alexa, launch Busuu Quiz Bot,” and you will have access to more than 1,000 questions that test your vocabulary and speaking skills.

Busuu's site: https://www.busuu.com/en


Benefits from AI in language learning

AI algorithms have the potential to advance eLearning in every field. Companies can use language learning solutions to train employees. Individual learners can use AI in language learning to study anytime, anywhere. Schools and colleges can employ it to increase students' opportunities. More importantly, AI has already proven its significance in linguistics.


Adapting to student needs

A key benefit of AI is the personalization of the learning experience. When teaching a class full of students, it's almost impossible for teachers to find an approach that suits all. When using AI for learning a new language, the needs of each individual student can be fulfilled. Educators can collect useful data about learners, their abilities, and their learning styles. When analyzed correctly, this data can predict future performance and pave the way for personalized education.


Providing instant feedback

With artificial intelligence in language learning, feedback is almost instantaneous. AI-based platforms grade tests and evaluate essays automatically after submission, pointing out errors and how to fix them. As a result, students can instantly take action to correct their mistakes and avoid them in the future.


AI language learning solutions also help teachers pinpoint weaknesses in curriculum. They can identify misleading questions, show how they can be improved, and identify who needs additional guidance.


Redifined roles for teachers

Not even the most sophisticated language learning bot can entirely replace human teachers. However, AI can redefine teachers' roles. AI algorithms can carry out grading, paperwork, and other mundane administrative tasks quickly and efficiently, allowing teachers more time to coordinate learning processes and mentor students. Tech-savvy teachers may also analyze data collected by algorithms.


In conclusion...

AI keeps evolving for either the better or the bad. In this case, AI can be a helpful tool to make learning much more easier and dynamic than the traditional way. Don't worry, teachers won't loose their jobs, but they can serve as mentors for those who require an extra help.


Maybe some people don't find using AI an efficient way to learn and still rather stick to the old learning method, everyone is different afterall, this AI technology is still available and keeps improving for the better.
